<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The semiconductor wafer used electrostatic chuck (ESC) market consists of two main types: Coulomb type and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type. Coulomb type ESCs utilize electrostatic attraction between the chuck and the wafer surface to hold the wafer in place. On the other hand, JR type ESCs implement an electrostatic field to generate a vacuum-like force, allowing for better wafer holding. Both types play a crucial role in the semiconductor industry by ensuring secure wafer fixation during the manufacturing process.</p></p>
