<p><p>Defense fuel cells, such as PEMFC (Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells), SPFC (Solid Oxide Fuel Cells), and DMFC (Direct Methanol Fuel Cells), are different types of fuel cells used in the defense sector. PEMFCs use a polymer electrolyte membrane, while SPFCs employ a solid oxide electrolyte. DMFCs, on the other hand, use a direct methanol fuel mixture. These fuel cells are valuable for defense applications due to their high energy efficiency, reliability, and ability to operate in harsh conditions. They offer a clean and sustainable power source for various defense systems, including vehicles and portable electronics.</p></p>
