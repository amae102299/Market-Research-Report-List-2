<p><p>Mechanical timers are devices used for tracking time in various applications. Manually clock timers require users to set and adjust the time manually. Spring-driven timers utilize a wound spring that gradually unwinds, releasing energy to measure time. Dashpot timers rely on the resistance of a viscous fluid to control the speed of a moving part, resulting in precise time measurement. The "Others" category encompasses a range of different types, such as synchronous motors, water clocks, and pneumatic timers, each offering unique mechanisms for timekeeping.</p></p>
