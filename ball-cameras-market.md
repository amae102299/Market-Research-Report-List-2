<p><p>Analog cameras capture and transmit video signals in analog format, using coaxial cables. They are typically used in traditional surveillance systems and offer lower resolution compared to IP cameras. On the other hand, IP cameras are digital devices that convert video images into data and transmit them over an IP network. They provide higher resolution, greater flexibility in terms of remote access, and advanced features such as motion detection and video analytics. IP cameras have gained popularity in recent years due to advancements in technology and the growing demand for network-based surveillance systems.</p></p>
