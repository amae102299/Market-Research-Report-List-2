<p><p>Ground Fault Sensors are devices used to detect ground faults in electrical circuits. They are available in two main types: AC Ground Fault Sensors and DC Ground Fault Sensors. </p><p>AC Ground Fault Sensors are designed to detect ground faults in alternating current (AC) circuits. They monitor the flow of current and compare it to the current returning through the neutral wire, triggering an alarm or shutting off power if a ground fault is detected. </p><p>On the other hand, DC Ground Fault Sensors perform a similar function, but they are specifically designed for direct current (DC) circuits. They monitor the flow of current and provide an alert or cut off power in the event of a ground fault.</p></p>
