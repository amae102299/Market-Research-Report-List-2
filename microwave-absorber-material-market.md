<p><p>Microwave absorber materials are used to minimize the reflection and transmission of microwave signals. These materials can be categorized into two main types: low permeability and high permeability. </p><p>Low permeability materials have a low magnetic permeability, meaning they have a weaker magnetic response to an applied magnetic field. This type of material is effective in absorbing microwaves by converting the electromagnetic energy into heat.</p><p>On the other hand, high permeability materials have a high magnetic permeability, meaning they have a stronger magnetic response. These materials are efficient in absorbing microwaves by redirecting the magnetic field lines, leading to greater absorption and attenuation of the electromagnetic energy.</p></p>
