<p><p>Food grade cellulose refers to a type of cellulose that is deemed safe for consumption in the food industry. Two commonly used types of food grade cellulose are Carboxy Methyl Cellulose (CMC) and Methyl Cellulose (MC). CMC is a cellulose derivative that acts as a thickener, stabilizer, and emulsifier in food products. MC, on the other hand, is primarily used as a binder and texture enhancer. Both CMC and MC play important roles in improving the texture, stability, and overall quality of various food products without posing any health risks.</p></p>
