<p><p>The Digital Visual Interface (DVI) market consists of three main types: DVI-A, DVI-D, and DVI-I. DVI-A refers to analog-only connectivity, allowing transmission of analog signals. DVI-D, on the other hand, is digital-only, enabling the transfer of digital data. Lastly, DVI-I stands for integrated and supports both analog and digital signals, providing more flexibility in connectivity options. Each type caters to different display and device requirements, allowing users to choose the appropriate DVI variant based on their specific needs.</p></p>
