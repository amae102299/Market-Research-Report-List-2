<p><p>LEDs, or light-emitting diodes, are categorized based on their emitted wavelength range. The 0 to 700 nm range represents visible light, covering colors like red, green, and blue. LEDs in the 700 to 1100 nm range fall in the near-infrared spectrum, often used in applications like remote controls or surveillance systems. LEDs in the 1100 to 1500 nm range belong to the short-wavelength infrared region, useful for night vision or short-range communication. Lastly, the 1500 to 1800 nm range is categorized as mid-wavelength infrared, utilized in applications like gas sensing or thermal imaging.</p></p>
